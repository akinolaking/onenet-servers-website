## BRAND VOICE RULES

1. Outcome-first. Lead with what the customer gets, not what the product does.
2. Short sentences. One idea per sentence. Break long sentences.
3. No adverbs. "Incredibly fast" → "Fast." "Highly reliable" → "99.9% uptime."
4. No corporate nouns. Not "leveraging", not "solutions", not "cutting-edge".
5. Address the reader as "you" and "your". The company is "we" or "OneNet".
6. UK English spelling: "colour", "organisation", "recognised".
7. Naira prices always in amber. GBP secondary. USD baseline.
8. Every CTA is a verb + noun. Not "Learn more" → "See all plans", "Start building", "Get started free".

### Transfer FAQ (7 questions)
Q: How long does a domain transfer take?
A: Most standard TLDs (.com, .net, .org, .co.uk) complete within 5–7 days. .ng and .com.ng transfers are managed by NiRA and take up to 14 days. We'll keep you updated at every stage.

Q: Will my website go down during the transfer?
A: No. Your DNS records remain active at your current registrar throughout the transfer. Once the transfer completes and you've pointed your nameservers to us, there's a brief DNS propagation window of up to 48 hours where some users may reach the old host — but your site stays live the entire time.

Q: What is an EPP/authorisation code?
A: An EPP code (also called an auth code or transfer key) is a password your current registrar issues to authorise a transfer. You'll find it in your current registrar's domain management panel. If you can't find it, contact their support team — they're required to provide it.

Q: Can I transfer a .name.ng domain?
A: No. .name.ng domains are non-transferable. They are issued under the Digital Identity Initiative and can only be managed within the OneNet Servers account they were registered in.

Q: Does the transfer extend my registration?
A: Yes. A successful transfer adds one full year to your current expiry date. If your domain was due to expire in 6 months, after transfer it will expire in 18 months — at no extra cost beyond the standard transfer fee.

Q: What if my transfer fails?
A: The most common reasons for failure are: domain locked at current registrar (unlock it first), incorrect EPP code, or domain registered less than 60 days ago (ICANN rule). We'll let you know exactly what happened and how to fix it.

Q: Can I transfer multiple domains at once?
A: Yes. You can initiate bulk transfers from our client area. Contact support for transfers of 5+ domains and we'll coordinate the process for you.

### FAQ — SSL (5 questions)
Q: Is SSL already included with my hosting plan?
A: Yes. All OneNet Servers hosting plans include a free Let's Encrypt SSL certificate that auto-renews every 90 days. It's installed automatically when you add a domain to your hosting account. You never need to think about it.

Q: What's the difference between free SSL and paid SSL?
A: Free (DV) SSL encrypts traffic between your visitor and your server — this is all most sites need. Paid OV and EV certificates additionally verify your organisation's legal identity, which adds a further layer of trust for visitors and may be required by payment processors or enterprise clients.

Q: Will adding SSL break my website?
A: No. Our team handles SSL installation and configuration. If your site has mixed content (HTTP images on HTTPS pages), we'll flag it and guide you through fixing it. In most cases, the transition is invisible to your visitors.

Q: How does SSL affect my Google ranking?
A: Google has confirmed HTTPS as a ranking signal since 2014. Sites without HTTPS may be shown as "Not Secure" in Chrome and Firefox. While SSL alone won't shoot you to page one, its absence actively hurts your standing.

Q: Can I get SSL for a domain I host elsewhere?
A: Yes. Paid SSL certificates from OneNet Servers can be issued and installed on any server, including those not hosted with us. You'll need server access to complete the installation, or our support team can guide you through the process.
